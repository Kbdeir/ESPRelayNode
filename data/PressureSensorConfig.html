4–20 mA Sensor

How this page works

Your sensor outputs a current between 4 mA and 20 mA representing the physical measurement. A resistor converts that current into a voltage the ESP32 can read.

MQTT Publish Topic

The address where readings are broadcast. Example: \controller\tank1

Value at 4 mA — empty

What the real-world measurement is at minimum signal (4 mA). For a water tank this is usually 0 cm.

Value at 20 mA — full

What the real-world measurement is at maximum signal (20 mA). For a 4-metre deep tank, enter 400 (cm).

4 mA Calibration

The raw ADC number when exactly 4 mA is flowing. Measure on your actual hardware — the calculator gives a rough estimate only.

20 mA Calibration

Same idea for maximum signal. The calculator gives a reliable estimate for this one.

Burden Resistor

The resistor that converts sensor current to voltage. A 150 Ω resistor turns 20 mA into 3.0 V, giving best resolution.

Calibration Calculator

Enter your burden resistor and ADC full-scale voltage (Vmax) to get estimated calibration numbers. Vmax is typically 3100–3300 mV; 3179 mV matches this hardware.

Reading when tank is empty (ADC at 4 mA). Usually 0.

ADC = round(I × R × 4 096 000 / Vmax)
With ADC_ATTEN_DB_11 the ESP32 ADC full-scale is typically 3100–3300 mV. 3179 mV is the calibrated value for this hardware.

Derive yours: Vmax = (R × 0.02 × 1000) × 4096 / ADC20mA.
