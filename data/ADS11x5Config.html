Help

What This Page Is For

This page configures the external ADS11x5 analog-to-digital converter used for more accurate analog voltage readings than the built-in ADC.

What It Measures

The ADS11x5 measures analog voltage on channels AIN2 and AIN3, scaled using the Divider Multiplier.

Common Uses

  • Battery voltage
  • Solar panel voltage
  • DC supply voltage
  • Analog voltage reduced by a resistor divider

How The Voltage Divider Works

Two resistors reduce the voltage before it reaches the ADS input. The firmware uses the multiplier to estimate the original voltage. For example, a real 24V input may be reduced to 0.76V at the ADS input, then scaled back to approximately 24V.

Important Settings

  • Resistor 1: upper resistor in the divider.
  • Resistor 2: lower resistor in the divider.
  • Divider Multiplier: scales the ADS reading back to the original voltage.
  • Calc: calculates the multiplier from the resistor values.

Disabled Fields

  • ADS Number: saved in config but not currently used to select an ADS module.
  • Relay 1 / Relay 2: saved in config but not currently used for relay control.

Where The Reading Goes

When power monitoring is enabled, the scaled ADS readings are inserted into the power JSON payload as Volts2 and Volts3.
